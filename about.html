About

Teach Reach is an Academic network where students and professors can exchange ideas and information quickly and conveniently. It allows instructors to communicate instantly with the modern student and it utilizes smart phone technology to facilitate student collaboration beyond the traditional lecture hall.

Teach Reach is a free to download; free to use, smartphone virtual classroom application, where anyone can become a teacher.

Dr Ira Richardson, professor of philosophy at UCLA, noticed how passionate students are about their smartphones. He pondered if there were benefits to integrate technology into his class. Dr. Richardson contacted his friend Vladimir Kudryavtsev, a technology enthusiast. Together they created the first version of Teach Reach.

The first version, Teach Reach v1.3, was able to support only one classroom. It was Beta tested in Fall 2013 Semester at UCLA. At the end of semester there were 8 unique users and 100% class participation. Students had positive reaction on how application was integrated into the lesson class subject.

Teach Reach v1.4 was used for Spring 2014 semester by Professor Wei in Beijing University (BNU)

Teach Reach has been growing as a project this past year. We recently released the newest version. You can find the app on Google Play or Apple App store.

What stands out about Teach Reach, is unlike other virtual classroom apps, Teach Reach is 100% free to download and free to use. It will always be free. We believe that education institutions should not be left behind from using useful technological tools (this case apps), due to no budget for high subscription costs other apps require.

Our side goal is to have 10k downloads in the first 3 months. By just downloading app, you are helping bring Teach Reach to education!
